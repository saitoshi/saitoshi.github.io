<p>When I entered the classroom on day one, I genuinely did not have any real strong idea about software engineering. I knew that they were people that created different programs that met the different demands and needs. And for me, that is something that I want to do. 
In the technical era, the majority of people are not going to be needed because humans are going to be replaced by AIs or machines. And since I wanted to survive through that I decided to take the data science route in the math department since it combined the math portion I liked, and the computer science that is needed in the future. Despite this course being a 300 level, to me it is still like an introductory course like a 100 level course. While the workload is more than that, so is the excitement of what I yet to know is too. 

<p> Additionally, I decided to take this course because of the professional skills that are taught. My friend who took this course told me that he enjoyed the different work done and that he recommended it because of the professional portfolios. He did not lie to me as we are only in the second week and have finished the first part of our portfolios. While they may still seem a little awkward, I am surprised by what I have done so far. And I want to keep going at my best gaining that harder work experience, so I can be closer to look professional since presentation and looks are everything to a person. 
 
<p> Furthermore, I wanted to learn JavaScript since that was the language that most companies required when I checked through a job listing website. While I self-taught the basics of the language, there was only so much I could do watching through YouTube videos and reading through books. Since my other friend told me that JavaScript is taught in ICS 314, I could have not waited for this course any longer. I worked hard through ICS 211 so that I could pass it and take ICS 314. And I plan to show that same or even better hard work in this course.

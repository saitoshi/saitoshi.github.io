With half of 2020 coming to an end, that also implies the 2020 spring semester coming to an end too. Initially, I did not have any strong knowledge about software engineering, but after taking the course I feel more confident. However, while the course taught us essential skills for software engineering, can they be applied for other subjects? The response to that question is yes. One of the major reasons is because Professor Phillip Johnson’s ICS 314 course is not your traditional college lecture course. Instead, it incorporates group work and communication methods that were frequent in primary school but functional for college students. The three main ideas to take back from this course was Project Management, Ethics, and Functional Programming. Despite this course centering on these topics around general software development, they can be taken outside from it.

### Project Management 
 
As the name implies, project management is the functional ability to work within teams. To conclude the semester, Professor Johnson broke us into teams of three to five people and had us use GitHub to manage within our teams. This was essential, especially during the COVID-19 lockdown.

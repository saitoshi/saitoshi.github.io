<p> Being a learning assistant requires both on campus and off campus work. The in campus work is where we assist and facilitate the lesson plans created by the professors and teaching assistants. The off campus part of the job is where we create worksheets or key guides using LaTex, so the students can succeed their math courses. However, the problems cannot be boring for the students and they have to be ones that will be useful for their exams.</p>

<p> Using Overleaf, I create both study guides and practice problem sets where it can be an aid to the students studying. Most of the times, the problems are based of the homework and the different comments of the professors and the teaching assistants. The worksheets I try to create them where they cover one topic each so that they match the textbook sections. A collections of the worksheets can be found from this <a href = "https://github.com/saitoshi/math241_242"> link </a>. 
</p>

<p> Creating worksheets taught me that creating problems is not an easy task. Each wording has to have clear meaning and the numbers used for the problems cannot be to difficutl. Most of the times, the worksheets are used by students who are not passionate and just there for the passing grade. Being a learning assistant alone teaches the skills to be patient with others and how to teach people in a clear manner. Worksheets gives the skills to create manuals for a new person at work. The skills to list words and directions in a way where majority can understand. </p>
